Bagnet

Bagnet is a Filipino dish featuring deep-fried pork belly, known for its crispy skin and tender meat. It's a delicious treat often enjoyed with rice and dipping sauces.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 2 cups water
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 tbsp vinegar (for brining)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine pork belly, garlic, onion, bay leaves, salt, peppercorns, and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour or until the pork is tender.
  2. Remove the pork belly from the pot and let it cool. Pat dry with paper towels.
  3. Marinate the pork belly in vinegar for 30 minutes, then remove excess moisture.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan or pot to 350°F (175°C). Carefully fry the pork belly in batches until the skin is golden and crispy, about 10-15 minutes per batch.
  5. Drain on paper towels and let it rest for a few minutes before slicing.
  6. Serve with rice and your favorite dipping sauce.
